At the budget and economy end of the Amsterdam short-term rental market, hosts are offering private rooms and part-time city apartments at competitive prices, with median prices ranging from €147 to €187. These segments, which include High-Turnover Private Rooms and Part-Time City Apartments, account for approximately 47% of the market, with the former typically accommodating 2.5 guests and the latter 2.2. The dominant room type in these segments is either private rooms or entire homes, with the majority of listings located in central neighbourhoods such as Centrum-West and De Baarsjes - Oud-West.

In the mid-market, segments such as Part-Time City Apartments and New & Unreviewed Listings are distinguished by their unique characteristics, including limited availability and a lack of reviews. Part-Time City Apartments, for instance, have a mean availability of 56.3 days per year, indicating that they are only occasionally available for booking. New & Unreviewed Listings, on the other hand, have a mean availability of 183.7 days per year, but have not yet garnered many reviews, with an average of only 0.3 reviews per month. These segments offer entire homes, often located in the same neighbourhoods as the budget-friendly options, but with slightly higher median prices, ranging from €187 to €241.

At the premium end of the market, Premium Spacious Apartments stand out for their high median price of €333 and ability to accommodate 4.4 guests on average. This segment is characterized by its high-end entire homes, often located in desirable neighbourhoods such as De Baarsjes - Oud-West. What makes the High-Turnover Private Rooms segment structurally unusual is its high turnover rate, with an average of 4.5 reviews per month, indicating a constant stream of new guests. As the Amsterdam short-term rental market continues to evolve, the data suggests that hosts and investors who can offer unique, high-quality listings with excellent reviews will be well-positioned to capitalize on the growing demand for premium accommodations.
