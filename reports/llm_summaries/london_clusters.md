In London, the budget and economy end of the short-term rental market is characterized by the Outer City Budget Rooms and High-Turnover City Lets segments, which offer affordable options to travelers. The Outer City Budget Rooms segment has a median price of £67.0 and accounts for 22.3% of the market, with typical accommodation sizes that can accommodate 2.2 people and often feature private rooms. The High-Turnover City Lets segment has a median price of £96.0 and makes up 14.5% of the market, with entire homes being the dominant room type and an average of 2.8 people accommodated.

The mid-market is dominated by the Standard City Apartments segment, which has a median price of £134.0 and accounts for 33.5% of the market. This segment is distinguished by its relatively low availability, with an average of 176.7 days per year, and its proximity to the city center, with an average distance of 5.2 kilometers. The New & Unreviewed Listings segment is also notable, with a median price of £121.0 and a high percentage of entire homes, but it is characterized by a lack of review activity, with an average of only 0.1 reviews per month.

At the premium end of the market, the Premium Spacious Apartments segment stands out, with a median price of £262.0 and an average accommodation size that can accommodate 6.1 people. This segment is distinct due to its high-end offerings and limited availability, with an average of 226.6 days per year. The data suggests that hosts and investors who can offer unique, high-quality experiences in desirable locations will be well-positioned to capitalize on the growing demand for short-term rentals in London.
