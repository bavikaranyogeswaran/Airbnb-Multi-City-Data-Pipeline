The London Airbnb market is substantial, with 96,871 total listings and 55,646 unique hosts. The median nightly rate is £135.0, indicating a relatively high pricing point for the city. However, there is a significant spread in prices, with the 25th percentile at £77.0 and the 75th percentile at £221.0, while the 95th percentile reaches £500.0, showcasing the diversity of options available to guests.

In terms of supply structure, entire homes make up 64.9% of the listings, while private rooms account for 34.7%. A notable 17.7% of hosts are superhosts, demonstrating a level of expertise and commitment to guest experience. The median availability is 96 days per year, and 26.9% of listings are highly available, with 270 or more days per year. This suggests that many hosts are actively managing their properties and seeking to maximize their rental income.

Guests are generally satisfied with their experiences, as evidenced by a median rating of 4.83 out of 5, with a median of 5 reviews per listing. The top neighbourhoods for listings are Westminster, Tower Hamlets, and Camden, indicating a high demand for accommodations in these areas. As the London Airbnb market continues to evolve, the data suggests that hosts who focus on providing high-quality entire home rentals in desirable neighbourhoods will be well-positioned to attract guests and drive revenue.
