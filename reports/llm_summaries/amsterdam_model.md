The LGBMRegressor model is trained on 4,757 listings and tested on 1,117 listings in Amsterdam, resulting in a test MAE of €80.7, which means that the model's predictions are typically within €80.7 of the actual price. The model explains 0.617 of log-price variance, indicating a moderate level of accuracy. Furthermore, 54.1% of the model's predictions land within 20% of the actual price, ranking Amsterdam as the 4th most accurate among the four cities. This suggests that the model is performing reasonably well in predicting prices for Amsterdam listings.

The model's performance is driven by factors such as the number of guests a listing can accommodate, the type of room, availability throughout the year, the number of bedrooms, and the listing's longitude. However, the model has a known limitation, as it systematically under-predicts the prices of luxury listings due to being trained on log-price, causing it to gravitate towards the market average in thin, high-price regions. As the model continues to be refined and improved, it suggests that hosts and investors in Amsterdam should closely monitor their pricing strategies to stay competitive in the market.
