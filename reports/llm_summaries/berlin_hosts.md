The dominant host type in Berlin is the Occasional Hosts, representing 59.8% of the city's hosts. This segment has a low superhost rate of 1.6%, indicating that they are not as experienced or dedicated to hosting. Their mean response rate and mean acceptance rate are 1.0 and 0.9, respectively, suggesting that they are relatively active in managing their listings. The typical availability for Occasional Hosts is 68.9 days, which is significantly lower than the other segments. This suggests that the platform in Berlin is relatively healthy, with a large number of casual hosts who are actively managing their listings, but may not be as committed to providing a high level of service.

In contrast to the Occasional Hosts, the Professional Superhosts segment is characterized by a much higher superhost rate of 67.5%. This segment represents 29.2% of the city's hosts and has a median average price of €101.0. Despite having a similar median listing count to the Occasional Hosts, Professional Superhosts have a much higher mean availability of 178.0 days and receive more reviews per month, with a mean of 1.8. The Occasional Hosts, on the other hand, have a lower median average price of €95.0 and receive very few reviews, with a mean of 0.1 per month. This distinction in behavior and portfolio size suggests that Professional Superhosts are more invested in their hosting business and are more likely to provide a high level of service to their guests.

The Passive Listers segment, representing 11.0% of the city's hosts, is characterized by low response and acceptance rates of 0.6 and 0.3, respectively. This segment has a high mean availability of 195.1 days, but receives very few reviews, with a mean of 0.2 per month. The data suggests that hosts in Berlin should focus on providing excellent service and maintaining high response and acceptance rates to attract more guests and increase their revenue.
