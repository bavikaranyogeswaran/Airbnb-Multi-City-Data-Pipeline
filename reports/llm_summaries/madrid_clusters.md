In Madrid, the budget end of the market is dominated by the Budget Rooms and Economy Apartments segments, which account for around 22.6% of the market. The Budget Rooms segment has a median price of €58.0 and typically consists of private rooms, with an average accommodation size of 2.3 guests and 1.1 bedrooms. The Economy Apartments segment has a slightly higher median price of €62.0 and is mostly made up of entire homes, with an average accommodation size of 1.8 guests and 1.0 bedrooms. Both segments have a significant presence in neighbourhoods such as Rejas and Embajadores.

The middle segments of the market are characterized by distinct availability patterns and distances from the city centre. The High-Turnover City Lets segment, with a median price of €105.0, has a high review activity and is mostly made up of entire homes, with an average accommodation size of 3.0 guests and 1.1 bedrooms. In contrast, the Part-Time City Apartments segment, with a median price of €108.0, has a lower availability and review activity, and is also mostly made up of entire homes. These segments are primarily located in Embajadores and other central neighbourhoods.

At the premium end of the market, the Luxury Listings and Premium Spacious Apartments segments stand out for their high median prices, ranging from €120.0 to €225.0. The Luxury Listings segment has a median price of €120.0 and is characterized by a high availability and a dominant presence of entire homes. The Premium Spacious Apartments segments, with median prices of €150.0 and €225.0, are distinguished by their large accommodation sizes, with an average of 4.4 to 6.4 guests, and a high percentage of entire homes. The data suggests that hosts and investors who can offer high-quality, spacious apartments in desirable neighbourhoods are likely to see strong demand and returns in the Madrid market.
