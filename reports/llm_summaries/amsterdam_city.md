The Amsterdam Airbnb market is sizable, with 10,480 total listings and 9,201 unique hosts. The median nightly rate is €222.0, indicating a relatively high pricing point for the city. However, there is a significant spread in prices, with the 25th percentile at €161.0 and the 75th percentile at €314.0, while the 95th percentile reaches €550.0, showing that some listings command much higher rates.

In terms of supply structure, the majority of listings, 81.7%, are entire homes, while 17.5% are private rooms. The superhost rate is 17.8%, suggesting that a significant proportion of hosts are experienced and highly rated. The median availability is 20 days per year, indicating that many hosts have limited availability, although 14.4% of listings are highly available, with 270 or more days per year. This mix of availability suggests a diverse range of hosting strategies in the city.

The guest experience in Amsterdam is generally very positive, with a median guest rating of 4.92 out of 5 and a median of 10 reviews per listing. The top neighbourhoods for listings are De Baarsjes - Oud-West, Centrum-West, and De Pijp - Rivierenbuurt, suggesting that these areas are particularly popular with guests. As the Amsterdam market continues to evolve, the data suggests that hosts who can offer high-quality, highly available listings in desirable neighbourhoods will be well-positioned to attract guests and drive revenue.
