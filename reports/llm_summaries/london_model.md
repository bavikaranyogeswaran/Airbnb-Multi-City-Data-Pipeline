The LGBMRegressor model is trained on 49,800 listings and tested on 12,163 listings in London, resulting in a test MAE of £77.5, which means that the model's predictions are typically within £77.5 of the actual price. This level of accuracy is notable, as the model explains 0.69 of log-price variance, indicating a strong ability to capture the underlying patterns in the data. Furthermore, 49.6% of the model's predictions land within 20% of the actual price, which ranks as the 3rd most accurate among the four cities being analyzed.

The model's performance is driven by several key factors, including room_type, accommodates, neighbourhood_cleansed, latitude, and bedrooms, which are the top features influencing price predictions. However, the model is not without its limitations, as it is known to systematically under-predict luxury listings due to being trained on log-price, causing it to gravitate towards the market average in thin, high-price regions. As the model continues to be refined and improved, it suggests that hosts and investors in London should be aware of these dynamics when using the model to inform their pricing strategies.
